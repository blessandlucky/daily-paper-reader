Abstract: This paper presents a high-order bound-preserving oscillation-eliminating discontinuous Galerkin (BP-OEDG) scheme for simulating gas-gas and gas-liquid two-phase flows governed by the Kapila five-equation model with the Tammann equation of state (EOS). The primary computational bottleneck arises from the severe CFL re-striction imposed by the stiff κ-source term in the volume fraction equation. To cir-cumvent this, we propose a novel operator-splitting strategy that decouples the system into a transport model and a stiff κ-source term. The former is discretized via a quasi-conservative DG method [8], while the latter is resolved by an adaptive implicit strategy hybridizing the backward Euler and SDIRK2 methods. We rigorously prove that this implicit treatment is unconditionally BP, effectively removing the stiffness-induced sta-bility constraints inherent in traditional explicit schemes. To further enhance precision, a velocity divergence reconstruction inspired by the Local Discontinuous Galerkin (LDG) method is integrated into the implicit solver. Furthermore, an OE limiter is employed to suppress spurious oscillations without characteristic decomposition, complemented by a BP limiter to ensure the BP property of partial densities, pressure, and volume fraction. Crucially, we prove that the proposed BP-OEDG scheme, integrated with the splitting strategy, strictly satisfies the Abgrall condition. Extensive numerical ex-periments, including challenging water-air shock-bubble interactions, demonstrate the superior robustness and efficiency of the method. 1

# 1 Introduction 

Multiphase flow simulation is a challenging problem in fluid mechanics, with appli-cations in various fields such as aerospace, energy, and environmental sciences. Many models can be used to simulate two-phase flows, and most of them can be classified as two-fluid models or as homogeneous models. Among the two-fluid models, the model of Baer and Nunziato (BN) [3] is firstly proposed to simulate the deflagration-to-detonation transition in reactive granular materials. The BN model includes mass momentum, and energy conservative laws for each phase and a volume fraction equation. Then, the Kapila five-equation model [15] has been derived as a reduced model under the assump-tion that velocities and pressures across different phases are in equilibrium. Compare to the five-equation transport model [2, 17], the Kapila five-equation model contains an additional non-conservative κ-source term in volume fraction equation. This additional term accounts for the different compressibility of two fluids. While this term enables the Kapila five-equation model to achieve higher fidelity than the five-equation trans-port model in bubble dynamics simulation [21, 29]. However, its inherent stiffness poses numerical instabilities when shock or rarefaction waves are hitting material interface. [20, 35, 4]. This presents a highly challenging problem for numerical schemes. To avoid such numerical instabilities, Saurel et al. [20] proposed a six-equation model featuring pressure relaxation. Nevertheless, the Kapila five-equation model is still widely used as a model equation due to its simplicity. In recent years, the DG method has gained significant attention in computational fluid dynamics due to its high-order accuracy, compact stencil, and excellent paral-lel scalability. Consequently, its application to multiphase flows has been increasingly explored. Zhang et al. [8] proposed a quasi-conservative DG method to solve the five-equation transport model with the Tammann EOS. They subsequently extended this DG framework to solve the Kapila five-equation model based on the ideal gas EOS [35], where the discretization of the non-conservative source terms only achieves second-order accuracy. To address this, Yan et al. [34] further developed a DG spatial discretiza-tion for the Kapila model that successfully attains optimal high-order accuracy for the source terms. Despite these advancements in spatial discretization, simulating gas-liquid two-phase flows using the Kapila model remains a challenge because of the stiffness of 

κ-source term. For instance, at the instant a shock wave in water impacts a air bubble interface, the velocity divergence at the gas-water interface reaches extremely high val-ues. If explicit time integration is applied to the volume fraction equation, the κ-source term can cause the volume fraction to exceed its physical bounds in standard CFL con-dition. To prevent such numerical instability, the CFL condition traditionally demands severe restrictions, often reducing the allowable time step by several orders of magnitude 2[35, 32]. The DG method equipped with subcell finite volume discretization proposed by White et al. [32], whose analysis of CFL condition for BP is similar to the theoretical limitations analyzed by Zhang and Cheng [35]. The BP CFL condition in these methods is heavily dominated by the stiff volume fraction equation. This inherent stiffness forces the allowable time step to shrink by several orders of magnitude compared to the normal CFL limitation. Simulating extreme gas-liquid cases, such as water-air shock-bubble interaction, remains computationally prohibitive and conspicuously absent from current DG literatures. To overcome this stiffness, attempts have been made using alternative approaches. Notable examples include the acoustic-convective splitting-based scheme by Eikelder et al. [28] and the second-order GRP-FVM with a semi-implicit time discretiza-tion for the κ-source term by Chen et al. [5]. However, addressing this stiffness through high-order DG methods has yet to be explored. Therefore, developing a robust, BP DG method for the gas-liquid Kapila five-equation model that can circumvent this extreme time-step restriction and operate stably under a standard CFL condition represents a highly non-trivial challenge, which forms the primary motivation of the present work. In this paper, to circumvent the stiffness-induced instability of the κ-source term, we propose an operator-splitting strategy in DG framework that decomposes the Kapila five-equation into a five-equation transport model and a stiff κ term. The five-equation transport model is discretized using a quasi-conservative DG method which satisfies the Abgrall condition. Specifically, we solve the stiff κ-source term using a novel adaptive implicit strategy—a hybrid of backward Euler and second-order SDIRK schemes that en-sures unconditional BP property. Furthermore, the velocity divergence is reconstructed inspired by the LDG method [10], thereby restoring optimal high-order precision. An OEDG framework is implemented to suppress numerical oscillations. A key advantage of the OEDG approach is that it effectively eliminates oscillations without the need for characteristic decomposition, which is typically required by traditional limiters such as WENO limiter [37] or TVD limiter [9]. This significantly reduces the computational complexity and simplifies the implementation for complex multiphase systems. To fur-ther ensure the BP property of critical physical variables including partial densities, pressure, and volume fraction, a robust BP limiter is integrated into the framework. We rigorously prove that this BP-OEDG scheme satisfies the Abgrall condition (maintaining pressure and velocity equilibrium at interfaces) for the Tammann EOS. The paper is organized as follows. We introduce the governing equation of the Kapila five-equation model in Section 2. Then, we present the operator-splitting DG method in Section 3, where we describe the splitting strategy, a specific implicit solver for κ-source term, and a quasi-conservative DG method for the five-equation transport model. The implementation of the BP limiter and OEDG procedure are presented in Section 4, where we prove the preservation of Abgrall condition for the Tammann EOS. The conclusion of calculate procedure is also presented in Section 4. In Section 5, we present 1D and 2D benchmark cases to verify the performance of BP-OEDG scheme combined with the operator splitting method. In Section 6, we give the conclusion. 32 Governing equation

# 5 Numerical experiments 

This section presents 1D and 2D benchmark cases to verify the performance of the BP-OEDG scheme combined with the operator splitting method. For notational convenience, our proposed framework is denoted as ‘Kapila-split’, while the direct DG method [35] used for reference solutions is denoted as ‘Reference’. For the 1D cases, we utilize reference solutions computed by the direct DG method on a sufficiently fine mesh. In 1D smooth test cases, the numerical errors and convergence rates are then evalu-ated for our proposed BP-OEDG scheme combined with the operator splitting method. To ensure a comprehensive verification, we employ both DG( P 1) and DG( P 2) spatial discretizations. For all cases, we simply employ ∆ t ∝ ∆x and CF L = 0 .1. Although this conventional scaling restricts the temporal accuracy to second order at the two-phase in-terface due to the operator splitting, such second-order temporal accuracy is practically sufficient for resolving the contact discontinuities in two-phase flows.

# 6 Conclusion 

In this paper, we propose a BP-OEDG scheme combined with operator splitting method for solving Kapila five-equation model to simulate compressible two-phase flow. The operator splitting method is used to address the stiffness-induced instability of the 

κ source term. Implicit solver is used in subsystem 3.23 to maintain the BP property of the volume fraction. And a quasi-conservative DG method is used in subsystem 3.21. The OEDG procedure and BP limiter are used to suppress numerical oscillations and maintain BP property of the solution. The proposed framework preserves conservation of the mass of each fluid, total momentum and total energy, meanwhile it satisfies Abgrall condition. While the proposed BP-OEDG scheme combined with operator splitting successfully stabilizes two-phase flow cases in the paper and locks the splitting errors at the inter-faces, We acknowledge that in certain extreme cases characterized by severe stiffness of the κ source term, the operator splitting method may suffer from local order degrada-tion [11, 25] and fail to restore the correct physical solution. Nevertheless, for the ex-treme compression dynamics targeted in this work (e.g., shock-bubble interactions), the proposed method strictly preserves the volume fraction bounded and maintains robust computational stability. To ultimately overcome splitting-induced limitation, developing 30 a fully coupled IMEX-OEDG framework remains a critical challenge and is the focus of our ongoing research.
