## 摘要
我们推导并分析了一个描述含表面张力的可压缩液体中可压缩气泡的时域压力-界面演化模型。从非线性两相 Euler 自由边界问题出发，通过在球形 Young-Laplace 平衡态附近进行线性化，我们得到了一个关于液体压力、气体压力和界面法向位移的耦合体-面双曲系统。由于表面张力二次型是不定的，时域分析变得复杂：其 $Y_0^0$ 分量是改变体积的呼吸模式，$Y_1^m (m = \{-1,0,1\})$ 分量是中性平移，只有高阶球面谐波才给出强制性的形状模式能量。我们利用了这种分解特性。强制性部分通过 $C^0$ 半群论证进行处理，而呼吸模式则通过 Fourier-Laplace 方法单独分析。这证明了在容许的有限能量数据下的适定性，以及在自然相容性条件下的经典解。随后，我们通过定量误差估计证明了两种极限描述的合理性。在声学准静态机制下，该模型退化为呼吸模式的线性化 Rayleigh-Plesset 方程和形状模式的线性化 Rayleigh-Lamb 方程。在另一种机制下，它退化为冻结界面声学传输模型。最后，频域分析确定了相应的 Minnaert、Rayleigh-Lamb 和 Fabry-Pérot 型共振机制是同一压力-界面表述的不同组成部分和极限。

## Abstract
We derive and analyze a time-domain pressure--interface evolution model for a compressible gas bubble in a compressible liquid with surface tension. Starting from the nonlinear two-phase Euler free-boundary problem and linearizing about a spherical Young--Laplace equilibrium, we obtain a coupled bulk--surface hyperbolic system for the liquid pressure, the gas pressure, and the normal displacement of the interface.   The time-domain analysis is complicated by the fact that the surface-tension quadratic form is indefinite: the \(Y_0^0\) component is the volume-changing breathing mode, the \(Y_1^m (m = \{-1,0,1\})\) components are neutral translations, and only the higher spherical harmonics give a coercive shape-mode energy. We exploit this decomposition. The coercive sector is treated by a \(C^0\)-semigroup argument, while the breathing mode is analyzed separately by Fourier--Laplace methods. This yields well-posedness for admissible finite-energy data and classical solutions under the natural compatibility conditions.   We then justify two limiting descriptions with quantitative error estimates. In an acoustic quasi-static regime, the model reduces to the linearized Rayleigh--Plesset equation for the breathing mode and to the linearized Rayleigh--Lamb equations for the shape modes. In a different regime, it reduces to the frozen-interface acoustic transmission model. Finally, a frequency-domain analysis identifies the corresponding Minnaert, Rayleigh--Lamb, and Fabry--Pérot-type resonance mechanisms as different components and limits of the same pressure--interface formulation.
