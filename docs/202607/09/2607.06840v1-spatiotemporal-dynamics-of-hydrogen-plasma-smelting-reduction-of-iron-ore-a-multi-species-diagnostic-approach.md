## 摘要
基于等离子体的矿物加工路径，如氢等离子体熔融还原（HPSR），能够在单一可扩展步骤中将铁矿粉直接转化为液态金属。这类工艺通常通过将电弧视为空间均匀热源来进行建模。然而，还原化学反应受等离子体-熔池界面强烈的非均匀条件支配，而空间平均诊断方法无法解析这些条件。本研究利用多物种发射光谱（OES）在空间和时间上解析了转移弧 HPSR 反应器的电弧，其中中性及离子态氩（Ar I, Ar II）、氢 Balmer 线和中性铁（Fe I）的发射光谱作为由其不同电离阈值设定的固有空间过滤器。结合熔池表面的红外热成像和针对基准自由燃烧氩弧验证的局部热力学平衡（LTE）热等离子体模型，测量结果揭示了一个强烈分层、非等温的放电过程：一个由氩定义的内核（>10,000 K）、一个部分复合的 Balmer 包络层（7,000-10,000 K），以及一个直接位于约 1,900-2,300 K 熔池表面上方的 Fe I 示踪界面边界层（3,000-4,000 K）。在这一剧烈的温度梯度下，正氢离子在到达表面之前发生复合，因此输送到氧化物的还原剂通量绝大部分是中性的，即原子氢（H）和振动激发态分子氢 H2(v)，而非通常假设的高能离子。测得的电子密度和激发温度界定了界面电离。这些发现重新定义了等离子体矿石还原动力学建模的边界条件，并建立了一个可推广至各类等离子体矿物加工系统的空间分辨多物种诊断框架。

## Abstract
Plasma-based mineral-processing routes, such as hydrogen plasma smelting reduction (HPSR), which converts iron-ore fines directly to liquid metal in a single scalable step are commonly modeled by treating the arc as a spatially uniform heat source. Yet the reduction chemistry is governed by the strongly non-uniform conditions at the plasma-melt interface, which spatially averaged diagnostics cannot resolve. Here we spatially and temporally resolve the arc of a transferred arc HPSR reactor using multi-species optical emission spectroscopy (OES), in which neutral and ionic argon (Ar I, Ar II), hydrogen Balmer, and neutral iron (Fe I) emissions serve as intrinsic spatial filters set by their differing ionization thresholds. Combined with infrared thermography of the melt surface and an LTE thermal-plasma model validated against the benchmark free-burning argon arc, the measurements reveal a strongly stratified, non-isothermal discharge: an argon-defined core (>10,000 K), a partially recombined Balmer envelope (7,000-10,000 K), and an Fe I-traced interfacial boundary layer (3,000-4,000 K) directly above a melt surface at ~1,900-2,300 K. Across this steep thermal drop, positive hydrogen ions recombine before reaching the surface, so the reductant flux delivered to the oxide is overwhelmingly neutral; atomic hydrogen (H) and vibrationally excited molecular hydrogen H2(v), rather than the energetic ions often assumed. The measured electron density and excitation temperature bound the interfacial ionization. These findings redefine the boundary conditions for kinetic modeling of plasma-based ore reduction and establish a spatially resolved multi-species diagnostic framework transferable across plasma mineral-processing systems.
